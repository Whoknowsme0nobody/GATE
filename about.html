About GATE

Global Academic & Thinking Exam - Breaking barriers in standardized testing

Our Mission

Education is the foundation of progress, yet access to fair and affordable standardized testing remains a significant barrier for millions of students worldwide. Traditional exams such as the SAT, ACT, and TOEFL present challenges due to their cost, complexity, and fragmented structure.

GATE was created to break down these barriers. As a nonprofit initiative, it offers an inclusive, integrated, and affordable testing solution that measures both academic knowledge and essential communication skills in a single, streamlined exam.

Our mission is more than testing; it is about empowering students globally to pursue higher education without the burden of exorbitant fees or systemic obstacles. Built by students, for students, GATE aims to redefine what standardized testing can be â€” accessible, equitable, and reflective of real-world skills.

Equity & Access

We're eliminating financial and logistical barriers that prevent millions of students from accessing higher education opportunities through our affordable pricing model and accessible testing platform.

Innovation

Our modular test structure with built-in breaks and integrated critical thinking assessment represents a fresh approach to standardized testing that better reflects real-world skills.

Community

GATE is built by students, for students. Our community-driven approach ensures that the needs and perspectives of test-takers are at the center of everything we do.

Test Structure

GATE is a 2 hour and 55 minutes exam designed to assess a balanced spectrum of skills necessary for academic and life success. The test is scored on a scale of 0 to 100 points, reflecting overall performance across all sections and question types.

Reading & Writing (65 points total)

Module 1 (30 points)

30 questions in 40 minutes

Reading comprehension, logical reasoning, grammar, and vocabulary

Break (5 minutes)

Rest period between modules

Module 2 (25 points)

25 questions in 41 minutes

Advanced reading comprehension and complex logical reasoning

Break (5 minutes)

Rest period between modules

Module 3 - Global Mind (10 points)

1 essay in 20 minutes

Critical thinking, ethics, and social awareness (scored but optional for university reporting)

Mathematics (55 points base + 5 bonus)

Module 1 (30 points)

30 questions in 40 minutes

Algebra, data analysis, and basic geometry

Break (5 minutes)

Rest period between modules

Module 2 (25 points)

25 questions in 41 minutes

Advanced algebra and complex word problems

Break (10 minutes)

Longer rest period between major sections

Module 3 - Advanced Question (Bonus)

1 optional question in 20 minutes

Calculus level mathematics (+5 bonus points)

Scoring System

  • Regular questions: 1 point each
  • Mathematics questions: 1 point each (+5 bonus for advanced question if attempted)
  • Global Mind essay: 10 points

Note on Development Status

The English Communication section has been postponed to Phase 2 development. GATE is currently in its initial development phase. The times and point allocations above are tentative and will be adjusted based on pilot testing and community feedback.
